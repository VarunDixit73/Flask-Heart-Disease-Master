What is heart disease?

Heart disease is a general term that means that the heart is not working normally. Babies can be born with heart disease. This is called congenital heart disease. If people get heart disease later, it is called acquired heart disease. Most heart disease is acquired.

Symptoms

A person can have heart disease and not feel sick. Some people with heart disease have symptoms. This is when there are changes or pain in the body to show a disease is there. Some symptoms of heart disease are: Pain in the chest—the heart muscle is not getting enough flow to keep it going. Trouble breathing—blood may back up into the lungs. Palpitations (a feeling that the heart is beating too fast, too hard, or not regularly). Swelling of feet or legs—blood is backing up from the heart into the lower body. Feeling weak because the body and brain are not getting enough blood to supply them with oxygen. Cyanosis (skin turning a blue colour) means that too little oxygen is in the bloodstream to supply the cells in the body.

Deaths

Heart disease is the biggest killer of both men and women in the United States, England, Wales, and Canada. For example, heart disease causes 4 out of every 10 deaths in the United States. This is more than all kinds of cancer put together. Also, one person dies of heart disease about every minute in the United States alone.
